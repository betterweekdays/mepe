Mary Ellen Pleasant Entrepreneur (MEPE) Fellowship
More Information

Overview

What is MEPE Fellowship about?

The Mary Ellen Pleasant Entrepreneurship (MEPE) Fellowship is a 10-week virtual internship with The Whether for Spring 2019.

Accepted fellows will be paid up to $2,500 and build a mini-startup focused on student coaching using Soft Skills and Values Assessment .

What commitments do I need to make for the internship?

Fellows are expected to commit at least 4 hours a week (about 45 minutes a day) to the internship. A 10-week curriculum based on key startup principles will be tied to campus engagement goals. It will also incorporate videos, readings, and quizzes.

What will I get out of the 10-week experience?

1. Practical experience working at a start-up you can put on your resume.

2. A way to help other students on campus be successful.

3. Leadership and team collaboration skills through the Soft Skills and Values Assessment.

4. A paid trip to an awards dinner celebrating your success and networking with other entrepreneurial professionals at the end of the internship.

What dates should I be aware of?

December 10, 2018 - Fellowship Application Deadline

January 14, 2019 - Fellows Announced

February 2019 - Internship Starts

How To Apply

Part 1 - Show Your Interest

1. Create an account on The Whether .

2. Favorite the 'Mary Ellen Pleasant Entrepreneur Fellowship' article in The Whether.

3. Complete your Soft Skills and Values Assessment in The Whether.

4. Submit Part 1 of your application .

Part 2 - Show Your Creativity

1. Convince at least 5 friends to complete their Soft Skills and Values Assessment.

2. Create a 2-minute video (YouTube, etc.) pitching your idea to help 500 students choose a major, pick courses, or prepare for an internship interview.

3. Submit Part 2 of your application .

Eligibility & Tips

1. Any student that attends an MEPE Fellows partner school may apply.

2. We recommend students who are exceptional, high-performing who will not graduate prior to Spring 2019.

3. We recommend students who have a minimum 3.0 GPA and have demonstrated leadership experience.

4. Students are encouraged to apply as a team (not to exceed 5 members). Each team member must submit their own application. The criteria in Part 2 of the application can include team members.

Mary Ellen Pleasant

Source: the Paris Review

Mary Ellen Pleasant is a very successful 19th-century African American entrepreneur, financier, real estate magnate and abolitionist who identified herself as ‘a capitalist by profession’ in the 1890 United States Census.

Pleasant came from humble beginnings being born into slavery, but together with her husband, through smart investments and business dealings, they would amass a fortune estimated to be around 30 million dollars at the time. She made active contributions to the abolitionist movement and fighting for civil rights in California. She also used her influence and wealth to employ Blacks (freed and runaways from the underground) at her various businesses.

She was a friend and financial supporter of John Brown, and was well known in abolitionist circles. After the Civil War, she took her battles to the courts in the 1860s and won several civil rights victories, one of which was cited and upheld in the 1980s and resulted in her being called “The Mother of Human Rights in California”.

The MEPE Fellowship embodies her vision, spirit, and desire to help others. By providing fellows an opportunity to showcase their entrepreneurial talent, fellows can make a significant impact on campus with other students.
